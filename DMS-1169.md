# DMS-1169: Remove `dms.DocumentChangeEvent`

## Context

The Change Queries redesign retires the unified `dms.DocumentChangeEvent` journal in favor of three purpose-built mechanisms tracked by other stories in epic DMS-1001:

- Per-resource `tracked_changes_<schema>.<resource>` tables populated by `*_Stamp` triggers (back the `/deletes` and `/keyChanges` endpoints).
- A `ContentVersion` / `ContentLastModifiedAt` mirror on every concrete `StorageKind = RelationalTables` root and on `dms.Descriptor` (serves `?minChangeVersion=X&maxChangeVersion=Y` filters).
- `dms.GetMaxChangeVersion()` reading `dms.ChangeVersionSequence` directly (DMS-1168, `/availableChangeVersions`).

With those mechanisms in place, `dms.DocumentChangeEvent` has no remaining responsibility. This story removes the table, its journaling triggers, and every test/fixture/manifest that asserts their presence.

**Out of scope (per ticket):** Updates to the normative design docs — `data-model.md` §1c and `update-tracking.md` §"Journaling for Change Queries". Other design-doc references to `DocumentChangeEvent` are likewise left untouched (per the team convention to keep deferral notes out of canonical specs).

## Testing Strategy

This is a **deletion** story; the bar is that removal must not regress what remains. Test coverage targets:

- **DDL unit tests** (`CoreDdlEmitterTests.cs`) — assertions of the form "table X is emitted" / "FK Y exists" / "index Z exists" / "trigger T is emitted" already cover every remaining object. Updating the alphabetical-ordering test and the three group-count tests preserves the invariant that the emitter still emits the full inventory minus the removed objects.
- **Golden SQL fixtures** — every fixture across `Fixtures/small`, `Fixtures/focused`, `IntegrationFixtures`, and `Fixtures/authoritative` is regenerated. The byte-for-byte diff (reviewed manually before commit) is the regression test: the only removed lines must be the six `DocumentChangeEvent*` artifacts and the `TF_Document_Journal` / `TR_Document_Journal` blocks.
- **Provisioned-schema manifests** — `provisioned-schema.{pgsql,mssql}.manifest.json` regen confirms that the actual schema introspection on a freshly-provisioned database no longer surfaces the table, its constraints, indexes, or the trigger function/trigger.
- **Profile-guarded-no-op suites** — `PostgresqlProfileGuardedNoOpTests`, `MssqlProfileGuardedNoOpTests`, and the ordinal-alignment counterparts continue to assert deep equivalence of pre/post state (root row, Document version/timestamps). Dropping the `DocumentChangeEventCount` field weakens the assertion only along the dimension that no longer exists; the remaining fields cover the "no mutation" invariant. No replacement assertion is needed for this story (per ticket scope).
- **Authorization write tests** — `PostgresqlRelationalPutAuthorizationTests` / `MssqlRelationalPutAuthorizationTests` continue to assert `ContentVersion` strictly increased, which is the substantive "write happened" check. The dropped `DocumentChangeEventCount.Should().BeGreaterThan(...)` was redundant with that.
- **Fingerprint + reference-resolver tests** — table-list equality assertions remain (just with one fewer element); these are the regression net against accidentally re-adding DocumentChangeEvent emission.
- **Build + format** — `dotnet build`, `dotnet test` on at least the DDL unit suite, and `dotnet csharpier format` on every modified `.cs` file.

No new tests are added by this story (a deletion that re-uses existing fixture infrastructure does not need new coverage; the regenerated goldens are the assertion).
